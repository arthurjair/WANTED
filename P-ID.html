The progress of this portfolio.

What I did

At the start of the semester, I aimed to refine and enhance the portfolio I had created previously. Inspired by Niccolo Miranda's Paper Portfolio, I envisioned a design that combined a wanted poster with a newspaper aesthetic, similar to the one from Prisoner of Azkaban:

I didn't hate this design, but it felt too monochromatic and text-heavy.

Tried a more modern design...

Then, I experimented with a more modern design, but it didn't turn out as well as I hoped, so I decided to abandon it.

Final Design

After my second assessment, one of my teachers asked me to discuss my design choices. He advised me to choose a single themeâ€”either a fancy newspaper or a Western wanted poster. I decided on the Western theme and created a moodboard to help refine the look I aimed to achieve:

moodboard






I created a Western-themed scenery with a red sky and a yellow ground dotted with cacti. I loved how this design turned out, especially the hand-drawn dirt, which added a dynamic element to the website. I am committed to continually improving this portfolio.

How it helped

This experience significantly improved my design approach for future projects. It taught me to seek inspiration, explore various ways to present elements, and use colors, fonts, and moodboards effectively. Feedback from peers and colleagues has been invaluable. Overall, this process has made me more thoughtful about how to start a design.
